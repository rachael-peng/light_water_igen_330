United Nations - Sustainable Development Goals (UN SDGs)

The UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) were established in 2015 to address key global environmental and societal challenges, and provide a general framework for improving the well-being of both our planet and the people on it by 2030. Each of the 17 objectives aims to bring light to a universal problem, why the problem is significant, and what the solutions are. These goals require global collaboration and serve as a call for action to commitment from individuals, governments, and organizations worldwide to protect the planet, end poverty, and ensure prosperity for all.

UN SDG #6

Clean Water and Sanitation: The objective is to ensure universal access to clean water and adequate sanitation facilities, as well as proper hygiene practices, to prevent waterborne diseases and promote health.

Our Connection to SDG #6

In many parts of Canada, including Indigenous communities, access to clean water remains a persistent challenge. Over 60 Indigenous communities across the country face ongoing water advisories due to issues like inadequate treatment facilities, contamination, and outdated infrastructure. This issue isn't limited to Canada; globally, around 25% of the population lacks access to clean and safely managed water, affecting a staggering 2.2 billion people worldwide.

Our project aligns closely with UN SDG #6, which focuses on ensuring access to clean water and sanitation for all. Through a dynamic art installation, our initiative aims to shed light on these challenges, spark public engagement, and advocate for solutions to improve water quality and accessibility.
